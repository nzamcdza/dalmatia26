Dubrovnik · 19–23 July

Casa Toni on Strma, 300m above Pile Gate. Everything below is on foot from there. Four nights, and the Old Town does most of the work for us.

Cruise ships rule the middle of the day. The town is ours before 9am and after 5pm. Monday and Thursday are the heavy ship days — each day card has the plan around them.

🏰 The walls & the big sights

City Walls — the one unmissable thing

€40 each, 8:00–19:30, enter at Pile (2 min from our door). Go at 8am sharp or after 5pm — midday is an oven with cruise queues. Walk it anticlockwise, against the flow. About 2 hours. Keep the ticket: it gets us into Fort Lovrijenac free for 3 days.

💡 We land on a rare no-ship Sunday. If there's any energy after check-in, walls at 5:30pm on day one is the play.

Buy online — shop.citywallsdubrovnik.hr

The Dubrovnik Pass costs about the same (~€40) and throws in Rector's Palace, the monastery museums and city buses. If we fancy any museum at all, buy the Pass instead of the plain walls ticket. dubrovnikpass.com

Srđ cable car — the view

€30 return each, runs 9:00–midnight. Sunset ~20:15 and it's the business up there — but so are the queues. Book a timed ticket online, or go mid-afternoon when it's quieter. dubrovnikcablecar.com

Lokrum island — half-day escape

€30 each covers the boat and entry, ticket valid 3 days. Boats from the old harbour at 09:00, then 10:00 and every 30 min to 19:00 — there is no 09:30. Returns 10:15 every 30 min to 19:15, plus a last boat at 20:00. Peacocks, monastery ruins, and the Dead Sea — a warm salt lake you just float in (lovely at 09:00, heaving by 11:00). The Iron Throne replica lives here too (Orlaith: photo). Buy online at lokrum.hr — the ticket desks queue mid-morning and shut at 18:00. Bring food: the island café is a bottleneck and no sunbeds are hired anywhere on it.

Worth it / skip it

  • Fort Lovrijenac — free with the walls ticket. It's the Red Keep. Go.
  • Rector's Palace — €15 (or in the Pass). Cool inside, one hour.
  • Franciscan pharmacy — €10. Oldest working pharmacy in Europe, lovely cloister. Quick.
  • War Photo Ltd — €10, open till 10pm. Heavy but brilliant. Perfect for the hottest hours.
  • Cathedral — free, stick your head in.

Sea kayak at sunset

€40–45 each, 2.5–3 hours around the walls with a swim stop, launching from Pile bay under our noses. July slots go days ahead — book straight away if we want it. Adventure Dalmatia

🐉 King's Landing (for Orlaith)

The free self-guided hit list:

  • Jesuit Stairs — the Walk of Shame steps. Early morning for a clear photo.
  • Fort Lovrijenac — the Red Keep (free with the walls ticket).
  • Pile bay — Blackwater Bay, right below the fort.
  • Minčeta Tower — House of the Undying, part of the walls walk.
  • Ethnographic Museum lane — Littlefinger's brothel exterior.
  • Lokrum — Qarth, plus the Iron Throne replica for the photo.

The tour: worth doing once — the guides have on-set stories and it orients the whole town. €22–30 each, ~2 hours. Top-rated is the King's Landing walking tour on GetYourGuide (4.9★); wallsofdubrovnik.com runs one at €22 from Pile Square. Do it day one or two, then revisit the good spots ourselves.

Trsteno Arboretum — the Tyrell gardens, 20 min up the coast by bus. Feels like walking onto the set. Half a day with travel — only if the fandom demands it.

🏖️ Beaches & pools — the full list

The rule for a hot day: in the water before 11:00 or after 17:00, under something between 12:00 and 16:00. The binding constraint here isn't the water — it's shade. Nearly every free spot below has none, and nothing to rent. Pick by "can we book shade for two", not by which beach looks best.

Walk from Casa Toni

Šulić (Kolorina) — 528m, 7 min. Rocky cove under Fort Lovrijenac, concrete platforms, ladders into deep clear water. Free, no sunbeds. Beach Bar Dodo on the cliff above does burgers €16.50–19, calamari €15, salads €15–16, beer €5–8, cocktails €9–16 — lunch and a drink each ≈ €45–55, cash only. Lounge/reggae by day. Get there 09:30–10:00 and claim a cliff terrace table — that table is the whole plan, it's the only shade.

Danče — 630m, 10 min west past the Franciscan convent. The oldest swim spot in the city, still overwhelmingly local. Flat rock and concrete, ladders into deep water, Jug water polo club's home. Free, no sunbeds, no hire. Kiosk does drinks only — bring food. Lifeguards 10:30–18:30. Shaded in the morning by Gradac park, full sun midday on. Best as an 18:00-to-sunset swim before dinner.

Porporela — 12–15 min, the Old Port breakwater. Not a beach: a concrete arm, locals diving, water polo. Officially monitored and rated Excellent across 40 samplings in 2025. Free, nothing sold on it, zero shade. This is an 08:00–09:30 swim before the day starts, or a 19:00 dip. Never a base.

Banje — 15–20 min out through Ploče Gate, ~100 steps down. The postcard one. Skip the free strip — most crowded free water in the city. 2026 price list: sunbed €30, umbrella €10, towel €5, Lounge Bed €90 (2 towels + 2 waters), Baldachin €100. The €90 lounge bed is the better buy and the only thing that can be reserved — reservations@banjebeach.eu, and collect it by 11:00 or lose it. Draught €8, cocktails €12–15, burger €19.50, sea bass €29, platter for two €76. Waiter service stops 18:00. It markets DJs but publishes no daytime schedule — don't promise ourselves a set. Go from 17:00 as an add-on, not as the day.

Short hop — bus or Bolt

Bus tickets: €1.73 from a Tisak kiosk in advance, €2.50 from the driver, €5.31 for 24 hours. Buy at Pile before boarding. Bolts can't enter the Old Town — walk out to the Pile/Brsalje rank, and supply is thin ~10:00 and ~18:00.

⭐ Coral Beach Club, Babin Kuk — bus 6 from Pile, 15–25 min, then a short walk down. Entry is free — walk in, swim, drink, pay nothing. Sunbeds €65–135 including towel, mattress cover and parasol, held all day; VIP double €170. (Baldachins €350 and lounge beds €500+ are minimum spends against food and drink, not rental.) Bar 09:00, kitchen 11:00, restaurant tables carry a €50pp minimum. Resident DJ daily — melodic, organic and afro house, genuinely Ibiza/Tulum rather than commercial; their own pages say 14:00–22:00 in one place and 13:00–midnight in another. There's a quiet zone with lower volume if we want the calm half. Best fit on this list. Book: +385 99 323 9430 — they say 24h notice but are advertising 30% off same-day double sunbeds.

Copacabana, Babin Kuk — bus 6, ~15 min (get off at Orsan for a nicer seafront approach). Big, facilities-heavy, watersports and slides, pine behind. Loud and family-heavy — the opposite of romantic. Free public section; deckchair + parasol for two €70 rental or €150 as a food-and-drink voucher, baldachin €120/€300, 3-hour open bar €85. ~10:00–21:00. Showers and toilets are paid — bring coins. Their web shop shows everything sold out (neglected shop, probably) — ring +385 99 424 8112.

Sveti Jakov — bus 5 or 8 to Viktorija, then 10–15 min walk and ~160 steps down. Or take a taxi boat from the Old Port and skip the stairs — that's the move. Prettiest proper beach, fine pebble, long view back at the walls. Free entry, sunbeds ~€45 the pair (sources conflict badly: €15pp, €25pp or €45 for two — bring cash). Beach restaurant rates 3.0/5 — fine for a beer, don't build lunch round it. Quiet before 10:30. Not a sunset beach despite what the blogs say — cliffs shade it morning and late afternoon, exposed at midday. The steps back up at the end of a 34°C day are the real cost.

Bellevue (Miramare cove) — 20–25 min walk past Boninovo, then ~100 steps down (the lift is hotel guests only — walk in via the Rixos side). Cliff-walled cove with a cave, fine pebble, unusually calm. Free, and no sunbeds for non-guests at any price — bring towels and our own shade. Nevera restaurant sits in the cave at beach level, 11:00–17:00 (+385 20 330 888). Faces south-east so it's in cliff shadow from mid-afternoon, fully shaded by 16:00–17:00. Good for taking the heat off, useless for an afternoon tan.

Zaton bay — 9km out on buses 12 or 15, 15–16 min. Long sheltered inlet, almost no tour traffic. Sunbeds, food and prices all unverified — a punt, but the best "quiet bay without a boat" candidate. Check Maps before committing.

Pools — read this before getting our hopes up

Nobody in Dubrovnik sells a proper hotel pool day pass. Rixos, Dubrovnik Palace, Excelsior, Bellevue, Kompas, More, Villa Dubrovnik, Grand Villa Argentina, Importanne, Royal, all four Valamars, the Sheraton at Srebreno — not one publishes a day-use rate. The aggregator DayPass has zero Croatian inventory (its "Dubrovnik" page actually serves Budva). The local position is that hotels turn non-guests away from pools June–September. Anything we get is a yes on the phone, priced on the spot.

  • NUKU Beach, Sun Gardens (Orašac) — the one guaranteed option. Public, verified: €50pp for a 15:00–18:00 slot, €110pp all day. The resort's own pools are guests-only, but a spa day pass exists for the indoor pool (price unpublished). Bus from Gruž, 25 min, drops on the main road above. +385 20 361 902.
  • Kalamota Island Resort, Koločep — adults-only, pool with sea views, beach lounge with canopy beds and cocktail service, 25 min ferry. Exact match for what we want. Day-visitor policy unconfirmed — worth the call.
  • Hotel Božica, Šipan — heated infinity pool 15m from the seafront terrace, private beach, food served on the beach. ~30 min boat from Gruž. Non-resident access unconfirmed.
  • Grand Hotel Park, Lapad — indoor and outdoor pools, spa, 100m from Lapad beach. Closest unexplored pool to us. Policy unpublished.
  • Hotel Mlini / Astarea — the cheap pebble-beach-plus-pool combo, ~7.5 miles, bus 10. Price unconfirmed.

Wildcard nobody suggests: a couple's spa afternoon — Energy Clinic inside Hotel Excelsior, or Rixos Premium's year-round indoor pool. Better on the romance-and-actually-relaxed axis than a fourth pebble beach, and it's weatherproof. Prices unconfirmed, ring first. There are no thermal baths or lido anywhere near Dubrovnik.

On the water

Lokrum — the shade answer. €30 each covers the return boat and reserve entry, ticket valid 3 days. Boats 09:00, then 10:00 and every 30 min to 19:00 (there's no 09:30); returns 10:15 every 30 min to 19:15 plus a last boat at 20:00. Pine and cypress right down to the rock — the only real natural shade near town. Dead Sea lagoon lovely at 09:00, heaving by 11:00. No sunbeds for hire anywhere on the island, bring water shoes and our own food (the restaurant's existence isn't confirmed, and there's one café bottleneck). Ticket desks shut 18:00 — buy online at lokrum.hr. Best played early or late: the 16:00/17:00 boat gets the emptying island and the soft light.

Lopud & Šunj — real soft sand, the rarest thing on this coast. Gruž → Jadrolinija line 807, €5.40pp each way, out 10:00 (arr 10:45) or 14:00, back 12:10, 14:50 or 18:45 last boat. Then 20–30 min walk to Šunj or a golf cart ~€3. Tickets are tied to a specific sailing — buy both legs at Gruž before sailing. The timetable owns the day, and the 10:00 puts us there with the crowd rather than ahead of it.

Cavtat — the romance play. Boats from the Old Town port (10–12 min from us, no Gruž involved), 45–60 min, three operators. Fares conflict — €10 one way on Adriana's site vs €15–20pp on the tourist board's; budget €20 each way. Rat Beach has real pine shade, showers and changing rooms, sunbeds ~€7 and umbrella ~€3. Žal, 10 min along, has the bars and music. Catch: last boat back is 16:15–17:30 depending on operator — confirm at the kiosk before sailing, or it's bus 10 from Gruž (€4).

Koločep — the low-faff island. Same 807 line, €5.40 each way, 25 min, and the sandy beach is a two-minute stroll from the jetty. Back 12:30, 15:40 (not 16:00) or 19:05. Couple of waterfront cafés and nothing else, busy with families in July.

BOWA, Šipan — the blow-out. Private canvas cabana in a cove, boat access only, €850 peak season for 1–4 people including a five-course chef's menu (drinks extra). Cheaper door: beach sunbeds €110pp full day or €50pp for a 12:00–15:00 or 15:00–18:00 slot, and that's not a minimum spend. Shuttle boat €25pp from Brsečine at 11:30/11:45/15:00, doesn't sail unless booked; private boat from the Old Port €987.50 return. Open 11:00–18:00. +385 91 636 6111.

Private boat: self-drive without a licence is a 5hp Pasara at €150/day — too slow for anything past Lokrum, no shade beyond a bimini. A skippered boat from the Old Port is €320–450+ for a 4–5 hour half day including skipper, snorkel gear, towels, chilled drinks and a paddleboard. We'd be paying for a twelve-seater, but it's the only way to get house music, sun and total privacy in one day. Bring a speaker.

Don't bother

  • Uvala Lapad / "Sunset Beach" — no concessionaire for the 2026 season (Croatian press, 30 March 2026). No sunbed hire, no facilities. Every English travel blog still describes a beach club there. It's gone.
  • Kupari — the famous ruins were entirely demolished; only the Grand Hotel shell remains. A pedestrian corridor reopened 16 July 2026 through a live building site, at your own risk. The reason to go has been bulldozed.
  • Pasjača — a rockfall buried the footpath in April 2025 and there's no confirmation it's cleared. No transport, ~200 steps each way, no shade or water, and no phone signal at the bottom — can't call a Bolt back.
  • Gradski bazen Gruž — the municipal pool's public swim slot closed 1 June for the season.
  • Betina Cave — boat or kayak only, a fixed 40-minute stop on a group tour, no toilets or food.
  • Nonenina — turns up on beach lists but it's a cocktail bar inside the Old Town. No beach, no swimming.

Sea ~26°C. All pebble, rock or concrete — the water shoes aren't optional, urchins are real. Bring cash: Dodo is cash-only, Copacabana's showers need coins, several kiosks won't take cards.

🍸 Nights

Buža II — the hole-in-the-wall cliff bar. Through a doorway in the south wall, drinks on the rocks, lads jumping off cliffs. Beer €6–8, cocktails €12–15, takes cards. Heads up: no sunset view — the wall blocks it. Go for the late-evening sitting, or early for a swim.

D'Vino — snug wine bar, Palmotićeva 4a. Do a flight of three Croatian wines. Pre-dinner.

Beer Factory — craft pints in a quiet courtyard when it's a pints night.

Culture Club Revelin — an actual club inside a fortress, festival season through July. Check the lineup — and take the promoter cards handed out near the square before paying full door.

Honest read: Dubrovnik isn't a club town. The night here is golden-hour walls → wine → Buža on the rocks. The big nights are Hvar's job.

Hvar · 23–27 July

Pharos Bayhill, a few minutes up the hill from the harbour, breakfast included. Three full days: boats, beaches and the best nights of the trip.

The hill: 10–15 min down to town, steps at the end. Sunčani Hvar porters meet every catamaran and buggy the luggage up free — message ahead (details in Bookings).

Worth knowing: Hvar brought in strict noise rules, so the scene has shifted — beach clubs peak at sunset, town bars go to ~2am, and the late-late stuff moved indoors. Still the party island, just a different shape to the night.

🏖️ Beaches & the town

Walkable beaches

  • Pokonji Dol — 20 min east along the coast path. Proper laid-back cove, beach bar, loungers €15–20 the pair. Gone by midday in July — go early.
  • Majerovica — 10 min west. Small, free, no fuss. The quiet-morning option.
  • Bonj (Beach Club Hvar) — the polished one, 15 min west. Standard bed ~€50; the over-water deck for two is ~€170 if we fancy one blow-out lounger day. Book ahead.

Pebble and rock everywhere. Water shoes. Urchins are real.

Fortica — the fortress

€10, open till 10pm. Steep 15–20 min climb from the square, so save it for the cool of the evening — head up an hour before sunset (~20:15), café at the top, the Pakleni islands laid out below. Best view on the island.

The square

Pjaca and the cathedral (€2, shoulders covered) — 20 minutes, done. This is a beach stop, not a museum stop.

On while we're there

  • Sat 25 — free choir concert on the square in the evening, part of the summer festival.
  • Sun 26 — Rambo Amadeus (cult Balkan act, a genuine curiosity) at Veneranda, 21:30. Tickets adriaticket.com.
⛵ The Pakleni day — the classic

Taxi boats leave the Riva every 20–30 min from ~9am. Cash. Last boats back ~6–7pm — check when you board.

The shape of the day: boat to Palmižana around 10 (€15 return) → swim in Vinogradišće bay → long lunch at Toto's (relaxed, good value — book: +385 21 718 242) → afternoon hop to Stipanska for Carpe Diem Beach — sunbeds ~€50, pool, nets over the sea; free entry, boats from in front of Mlinar bakery (€10 return). Book beds a few days out on WhatsApp: +385 99 446 8468. Back on the last boat, sunset drink in town.

Fancier lunch at Palmižana: Laganini — €40–45 a head, minimum spends on the beds.

Want nothing at all? Mlini or Ždrilca coves, €10 return.

Splurge alternative: skippered boat for the day, €500–800. Only if the mood takes us — the taxi boats do the job.

🎉 Nights out — the party stop

The circuit, in order

Hula Hula — THE sunset session. 15–20 min along the coast path west. DJ builds from 4pm, peaks 6–8pm as the sun drops behind the islands. Beds €15 on the rocks or €40 on the dock with service; cocktails €12–22. Standing room is free but be there before 4 for a bed. Winds down ~8:30 — it's the warm-up, not the night.

Kiva Bar — the alleyway institution, 2 min off the harbour. Free in, drinks €5–8, rammed from 10pm, closes ~2am now. Treat it as a loud, brilliant 45-minute scoop, not the whole night — it's tiny.

Carpe Diem bar (the harbour one) — the polished cocktail stop at the end of the Riva. €15–22 a drink, smart-casual after dark. Warm-up or wind-down.

Central Park Club — live band nightly from 8, just off the square. Cocktails €12–18. Music you can still talk over — the reset stop.

Ka'lavanda — lavender-lit cocktail lounge near the cathedral, open till midnight. Pre-dinner one.

Late-late — the after-2am scene has moved indoors: Pink Champagne (roughly midnight–6am, cover €20–50, burlesque show ~2am) and Passarola, a deep-house basement doing headphone "silent set" nights from ~1am. Check their Instagrams on the day for what's on.

⚠️ The famous Carpe Diem island boat party (1am–5am on Stipanska) has been suspended since 2024 and hadn't restarted at time of writing. The daytime/sunset "Afterbeach" (4pm–midnight) runs as normal. If the boat party matters, WhatsApp them — +385 99 446 8468 — and ask.

How the nights fall

  • Thu 23 (we arrive 18:25) — easy one: dinner, Riva stroll, one Kiva scoop to say hello.
  • Fri 24 + Sat 25 — the big two. Full circuit: Hula Hula sunset → dinner → Kiva → Carpe Diem bar → late-late if standing.
  • Sun 26 — town winds down earlier; Fortica sunset + Rambo Amadeus is the classier finish.

Split & Solin · 27–31 July

Two nights at the Radisson with its beach club, then two winding down at Salona Palace in Solin — spa, garden, Roman ruins next door, and nearer the airport for the early flight.

The Radisson's own beach club, downstairs on the pebbles. Free sunbeds for hotel guests — claim them by mid-morning, they go. Open ~9am–8pm, restaurant from noon, DJ through the afternoon till close. If we want the posh corner: pebble-beach cabana for two, €100/day — events.mistral@radissonblu.com or +385 99 168 1454.

Who's playing (confirmed off their events page)

  • Mon 27 – Wed 29 — resident DJ Jorgos Mitropetros, daily.
  • Thu 30 — The Illusionist: Matthew Bee & DJ Black Shark.
  • Fri 31 — The Illusionist by Tom Novy, with Jorgos & Monkey Miro.

Yes — the two headline nights land after we've moved to Solin. Thursday's is a €15ish Bolt away if we want it. Friday is Niall's call (see the last day).

Kašjuni + Joe's — the Solin days

Salona Palace runs a free shuttle to Kašjuni beach in July — get times from reception at check-in. Kašjuni is the nicest beach of the trip: pines behind it for actual shade, calm clear water. Joe's beach lounge holds the east end — beds €50–150 with full service, or lay a towel free on the open section. It fills by 10am, so morning it is. Bolt back ~€5–10 if the shuttle timing's off.

Bačvice, Split's famous sandy beach, is a culture stop not a beach upgrade — watch the locals play picigin for half an hour if we're passing on the way into town, and leave it at that.

🏛️ The palace & the Old Town — one relaxed outing

Split's Old Town IS Diocletian's Palace — a Roman palace with a city grown inside it. One good half-day, timed for the evening, covers it.

The shape of it

  • Go in around 5–6pm when the heat breaks and the korzo (evening promenade) starts.
  • The cellars (~€8) — vaulted Roman halls under the palace, naturally cool, and yes: the dragon-egg room for Orlaith. The one thing worth doing in the heat.
  • Peristyle — the central square, free. Sphinx included.
  • Temple of Jupiter (~€3) — 15 minutes, worth it.
  • Bell tower — 183 exposed steps. Only at 8am, or not at all. Skippable.
  • Riva at golden hour — the seafront promenade doing its thing. Free and the best bit.

Buy tickets at the Peristyle kiosks on the day — combos run ~€15–20 for the lot. Green Market's worth a glance if we're there in a morning instead.

Getting there: Bolt from the Radisson ~€6–8 (4 min), or a flat 35–40 min coast walk via Firule and Bačvice — the walk in for dinner, Bolt home, is the move.

✈️ Salona, the spa & the last day

Salona ruins — literally next door

The ruins of the Roman capital of Dalmatia start 200m from the hotel — amphitheatre that held 17,000, city walls, the lot. €8 in, open 9am–8pm, ticket office at the Tusculum entrance. Barely any shade and rough paths, so it's a 9am job with proper shoes, an hour or so. Genuinely great, and we'll have it nearly to ourselves.

The hotel

Thermae Salonae spa — pools, three saunas, steam. Ask at check-in what's included and book any treatments early. One to know: the hotel does weddings — we've already emailed asking if one's on for our dates; if reception confirms one, ask for a room on the far side.

Friday 31 — the exit plan

Orlaith: car at 06:45 for the 09:05 flight. Book it Thursday night on Bolt Reserve (schedule-ahead — don't chance a live hail at 6am in Solin), ~€15–25. Hotel's shuttle (~€30 a car) is the backup — ask reception Thursday.

Niall: checkout's 11:00 — they'll store the bags and reception are sound about letting you use the pool after. The smart day: Trogir. It's 20 minutes away on the airport side — tiny Venetian old town, Kamerlengo fortress, lunch on the harbour at Franka or Piccolo Ponte, and the airport's then a 10-minute hop. Bus 37 from Sveti Kajo (220m from the hotel) goes Solin → airport → Trogir every 10–20 min for ~€2 if the bags are light, otherwise Bolt.

💶 Money — don't get stung

  • It's the euro. Cards fine nearly everywhere; keep €50-ish cash for taxi boats, markets and small konobas.
  • Never touch a Euronet ATM. Bank ATMs only: ZABA, PBZ, Erste, RBA.
  • Any machine offering "pay in EUR or your currency" — always EUR. Same on card terminals.
  • Tipping: 10% at restaurants if it was good, round up coffees and taxis. Tip in cash even when paying by card.

☀️ Heat, sea & staying right

  • 28–33°C daily, savage UV. Factor 50, reapply after swims.
  • Shade or indoors 12–4pm. Mornings and evenings are when Croatia is lovely.
  • 2–3 litres of water a day each. Tap water is grand everywhere we're staying.
  • Sea ~25°C. Every beach is pebbles — water shoes on, urchins live in the rocks.
  • Phones: Irish SIMs roam free in the EU. Nothing to sort.
  • Plugs are round two-pin — Irish plugs need adapters. Bring two.
  • Pharmacy = ljekarna, usually 8–8 weekdays. Emergencies: 112.
  • Churches: shoulders and knees covered or you're not getting in.
